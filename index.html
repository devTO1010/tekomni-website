Three SMBs hit common operational friction. Three custom rebuilds shipped and running. Here’s what each one was draining, what got built, and what came back to the owner.

01 Regional trucking fleet
Dispatch was the bottleneck. Now it isn’t.

Every load assignment, every reroute, every HOS check ran through one human. Late pickups and compliance violations were caught after the fact — sometimes at 2 a.m. I rebuilt the dispatch flow around an AI assignment engine that weighs hours-of-service, equipment, weather, and customer windows on every load, and surfaces exceptions before they escalate.

Routing decisions: hours → minutes
2 a.m. exception calls: eliminated
Compliance violations caught before they happen — not after
02 Commercial electrical contractor
Estimates ate Sundays. Now they don’t.

Walking the site by day was fast. The write-up at night was what killed evenings — copy-pasting catalog SKUs, reconciling labor against role rates, formatting quotes. Customers waited days for proposals. I built a voice-first estimating system: dictate the scope on-site, the system matches the QuickBooks catalog and labor rates, the priced PDF lands in the customer’s inbox before you’re back in the truck.

Quote turnaround: hours, not days
Sundays reclaimed
Higher close rate — speed wins deals while scope is fresh
03 SMB marketing operations
Marketing was always “next week.” Now it just runs.

The owner knew weekly posting mattered — for hires, for buyers, for staying top-of-mind. But doing it meant becoming a part-time copywriter, designer, and scheduler. The agency alternative was $2–5k/mo for generic posts. I built a publishing system that learns the brand voice once, generates platform-specific content on a schedule, and asks for nothing more than a five-minute weekly approval.

5 minutes a week instead of three hours
$2–5k/mo agency invoice eliminated — without losing brand voice
Channels stay live — no more “I’ll get to it next week”

How I work

Discover → Build → Operate → Improve.

A continuous loop, not a project handoff. Most automation projects die because the consultant ships the system and disappears. I run it after launch — so it gets better while you sleep.

01 / Discover
Find the drain.

Walk the operation. Watch where hours go that shouldn’t. Identify the friction point that’s worth removing first.

02 / Build
Ship the fix.

Custom system designed around your actual workflow — not a template, not a SaaS subscription. Your data, your rules, your voice.

03 / Operate
Run it for you.

Monitoring, uptime, exception handling, vendor management. You don’t inherit a maintenance burden when I leave — because I don’t leave.

04 / Improve
Keep tightening.

Watch the system in production. Spot the next friction point. Tighten the loop. Then start again at Discover.

“I don’t hand you the keys and walk away. I run it. I improve it. The loop doesn’t stop.”
